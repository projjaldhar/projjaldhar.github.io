Overview

Fleet electrification is one of the biggest operational transitions fleet managers face — but most are making EV purchasing decisions based on intuition, vendor claims, or generic industry benchmarks rather than their own fleet's actual usage patterns. The result is expensive mismatches: EVs purchased for routes that exceed their range, or combustion vehicles kept when EVs would already be more economical.

The EV Suitability Tool changes that by analysing each vehicle's real operational data — daily mileage, route profiles, dwell times, and charging opportunity windows — to generate a per-vehicle suitability score and ROI projection, giving fleet managers the evidence they need to make confident electrification decisions.

The Problem

EV decisions made on instinct

Fleet managers lacked a structured, data-backed way to evaluate which vehicles were genuinely suitable for EV replacement versus which would fail on range.

Unclear ROI on electrification

Without fuel cost vs. charging cost modelling per vehicle, CFOs couldn't build business cases for EV investment with confidence.

Range anxiety blocking adoption

Uncertainty about whether EVs could handle real-world routes caused hesitation, even for fleets where the data would show a clear green light for transition.

Solution & Architecture

The tool processes each vehicle's historical telematics data through an analysis pipeline that evaluates daily mileage distribution, route length profiles, overnight dwell patterns (charging opportunity), and operational schedules. This feeds a scoring model that outputs a suitability rating, estimated annual fuel savings, and a payback period estimate per vehicle.

Key Features

  • Per-vehicle suitability score — each vehicle gets a clear green/amber/red rating based on its actual usage pattern vs. EV capability thresholds
  • ROI and payback modelling — annual fuel savings, total cost of ownership comparison, and estimated payback period calculated per vehicle using real consumption data
  • Charging opportunity analysis — identifies overnight dwell windows and depot return patterns to assess whether the operational schedule supports charging without route disruption
  • Fleet-level summary view — aggregated view showing what percentage of the fleet is EV-ready today, what's borderline, and what needs further infrastructure before transition
  • Exportable reports — PDF and CSV export of suitability analysis for sharing with CFOs, procurement teams, and EV vendors during purchasing decisions

Impact & Outcomes

The EV Suitability Tool won the 2024 autoXreport Innovator Award and drove +15 daily active users within the first quarter post-launch. More significantly, it opened a new commercial conversation with fleet managers who were previously passive — giving the sales team a data-led entry point for EV transition discussions.

The product validated a key hypothesis: fleet managers don't resist EV adoption — they resist making expensive decisions without evidence. When the tool showed clear suitability and a credible payback period, adoption conversations moved significantly faster.

Lessons Learned

  • Sustainability sells when backed by ROI — environmental messaging alone didn't move fleet managers. Pairing it with a concrete payback period and cost savings figure changed the conversation entirely.
  • The CFO is the real user — fleet managers loved the tool, but the decisions were made by finance. Building an exportable, boardroom-ready report format increased the tool's influence beyond the platform UI.
  • Charging infrastructure is the hidden blocker — the tool surfaced cases where the vehicle was range-suitable but depot charging wasn't available. Adding an infrastructure readiness flag to the output prevented false-positive recommendations.
  • Starting with a clear green/amber/red score was the right call — early prototypes showed raw metrics only. Adding a simple three-tier verdict as the primary output dramatically reduced time-to-insight for fleet managers reviewing large fleets.
