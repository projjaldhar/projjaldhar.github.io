Product-led Certification

Pendo's certification in product-led growth principles — covering in-app guidance, user activation, feature adoption, and data-driven product decisions.

Pendo's Product-led Certification is grounded in the idea that the product itself — not marketing or sales — is the primary driver of acquisition, expansion, and retention. For B2B SaaS product managers, understanding this model is increasingly essential.

The programme covers both the strategic framing of product-led growth (PLG) and the tactical mechanics of how to implement it using behavioural analytics, in-app experiences, and product usage data.

Core Concepts

Product-led growth fundamentals — the distinction between sales-led, marketing-led, and product-led motions; when PLG makes sense and when it doesn't; how freemium and free-trial models shape product design decisions.

User activation and onboarding — how to define activation milestones, design onboarding flows that reduce time-to-value, and measure whether new users reach the "aha moment" that predicts long-term retention.

Feature adoption analytics — using behavioural data to understand which features drive engagement, which are underused despite being heavily built, and how to prioritise the roadmap based on what users actually do rather than what they say.

In-app guidance — designing tooltips, walkthroughs, and contextual nudges that help users discover value without requiring support intervention. The emphasis is on progressive disclosure: showing users the right information at the right moment in their journey.

Application to My Work

At Verizon Connect, most of our product motion is sales-led — enterprise deals, long implementation cycles, dedicated customer success teams. But the principles of PLG have direct relevance even in that context: the best enterprise products reduce the friction between a user logging in and getting value.

The CPRA notification panel I built applied several PLG-adjacent ideas: consent-aware messaging that felt helpful rather than intrusive, progressive disclosure of privacy settings, and activation nudges that increased engagement without increasing noise. The 8.3% monthly engagement lift post-launch was, in part, a product of thinking about the user journey in PLG terms.

Pendo's certification sharpened how I think about adoption curves, feature stickiness, and the difference between features users try once and features that become habitual — which is ultimately what separates a good product from a great one.
