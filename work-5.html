Overview

Staples.com required an order management system capable of supporting the future scale of their e-commerce business — handling millions of orders across their NAD (North American Division) Strategic Business Unit. The existing infrastructure lacked the flexibility and scalability that enterprise-grade Sterling OMS could provide.

As part of the Infosys delivery team, I worked on the implementation of IBM Sterling's YOMS (Your Order Management System) and OFL-LCA (Order Fulfilment Logic – Lifecycle and Availability) components, covering the full spectrum from PO creation and management through to supply collaboration and transfer order workflows.

The Problem

Scalability ceiling

The existing OMS was not built to handle the projected growth of Staples.com's e-commerce volume, creating a strategic risk for the business.

Supply chain visibility gaps

Purchase order management, transfer orders, and supplier collaboration were handled in disconnected systems with no unified view.

Complex fulfilment logic

Lifecycle and availability calculations for a retailer of Staples' breadth required a sophisticated, configurable rules engine rather than custom-built logic.

Solution & Architecture

The implementation leveraged IBM Sterling's proven OMS framework, configured for Staples.com's specific business rules. The YOMS component handled customer-facing order management lifecycle, while OFL-LCA managed the availability and fulfilment routing logic. The Supply Collaboration module connected Staples to its supplier network for PO, transfer, and removal order management.

Key Features

  • YOMS order lifecycle management — full customer order lifecycle from capture through fulfilment, modification, and cancellation with configurable business rules
  • OFL-LCA availability engine — sophisticated fulfilment logic calculating real-time availability, sourcing rules, and delivery promise dates at order capture
  • Purchase order management — end-to-end PO creation, approval workflows, and supplier acknowledgement through the Supply Collaboration module
  • Transfer & removal order processing — inter-warehouse transfer orders and inventory removal orders integrated into the unified supply chain workflow
  • Java API customisation — bespoke business logic implemented via Sterling's Java API framework to meet Staples-specific process requirements beyond out-of-the-box configuration

Impact & Outcomes

The Sterling OMS implementation provided Staples.com with a scalable, enterprise-grade order management foundation built to support future growth. The phased delivery approach — implementing YOMS and OFL-LCA first before extending to supply collaboration — allowed the business to go live with core functionality while the broader implementation continued.

Working on a deployment of this scale — for one of the largest office retail e-commerce operations in the US — gave me a deep understanding of how complex enterprise order management systems are designed, configured, and tested, forming the technical foundation that I later applied as a Product Owner across OMS and supply chain domains.

Lessons Learned

  • Configuration before customisation — every customisation added long-term maintenance burden. Exhausting Sterling's native configuration options before writing custom Java saved significant rework in later implementation phases.
  • High-level design documents are an investment, not overhead — the discipline of producing HLD and DLD before build caught architectural misalignments early, when they were cheap to fix rather than expensive to unwind mid-implementation.
  • Phased delivery de-risks enterprise implementations — shipping YOMS and OFL-LCA as a first phase before adding supply collaboration allowed the client to validate core functionality and build confidence before expanding scope.
  • Enterprise client expectations need active management — Staples had strong opinions about implementation approach. Regular structured communication on scope, progress, and trade-offs was as important as the technical delivery itself.
