Scrum Fundamentals Certified (SFC)

A foundational certification in Scrum and agile delivery principles — covering roles, ceremonies, artefacts, and how teams organise iterative product development.

The Scrum Fundamentals Certified (SFC) programme by SCRUMstudy provides a structured introduction to the Scrum framework — the most widely adopted agile methodology in software product development. While many practitioners absorb Scrum informally through experience, the certification forces a precise understanding of the framework's mechanics.

What Scrum Covers

Roles — the three core Scrum roles and the accountability each carries: the Scrum Master as servant-leader and impediment remover, the Product Owner as value maximiser and backlog custodian, and the Development Team as the self-organising unit responsible for building the product increment.

Ceremonies — the five Scrum events and their distinct purposes: Sprint Planning to align on what the team commits to delivering; the Daily Scrum to synchronise on progress and surface blockers; the Sprint Review to inspect the increment and adapt the backlog; the Sprint Retrospective to improve the team's process; and the Sprint itself as the time-boxed container for all of this work.

Artefacts — the Product Backlog as the single source of prioritised work, the Sprint Backlog as the team's committed plan for the sprint, and the Increment as the potentially shippable output of each sprint cycle.

How I Apply It

As a Product Owner at Verizon Connect, I operate within a Scrum framework daily. The SFC certification grounded the informal practices I'd developed — backlog refinement, acceptance criteria, sprint reviews — in the formal framework they come from.

The most useful shift the certification reinforced was around the Product Owner's role in backlog ordering. Prioritisation is not just about effort and impact — it's about sequencing work so that the team always has a clear, refined set of items ready for the next sprint, and that dependencies are visible and managed in advance.

Working across AI/ML, compliance, and fleet analytics features, this discipline of maintaining a well-ordered, well-groomed backlog is what keeps complex, multi-dependency roadmaps moving without constant fire-fighting.
