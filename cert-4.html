IBM Certified Solution Designer — Sterling Order Management V9.2

IBM's professional certification validating expertise in designing and architecting enterprise order management solutions on the Sterling OMS platform.

The IBM Certified Solution Designer — Sterling Order Management V9.2 is an advanced practitioner certification that validates deep expertise in IBM Sterling's enterprise OMS platform. Earning it required demonstrating proficiency across the full solution design lifecycle: requirements gathering, high-level and detailed design, configuration, and integration architecture.

I earned this certification during my time as a Package Solution Consultant, after several years of hands-on Sterling OMS implementations for major US retailers including Staples.com, Kohls, and Williams Sonoma.

What the Certification Validates

Solution architecture — designing end-to-end OMS solutions that handle order capture, inventory availability, fulfilment routing, and supply chain collaboration at enterprise scale. This includes understanding the trade-offs between Sterling's out-of-the-box capabilities and custom extensions.

Order fulfilment design — configuring order orchestration pipelines, sourcing rules, and fulfilment logic that handle complex scenarios: split shipments, store fulfilment, vendor drop-ship, return authorisation, and exchange flows across distributed inventory networks.

Integration architecture — designing interfaces between Sterling OMS and external systems including ERP, WMS, POS, and e-commerce platforms. Understanding API design, event-driven messaging, and data transformation between heterogeneous enterprise systems.

Configuration and customisation — knowing where to configure versus where to extend the Sterling platform, and the long-term maintainability implications of each approach. Custom APIs, agent pipelines, and service extensions all carry different costs across the system lifecycle.

Why It Mattered

IBM Sterling OMS underpins the order management infrastructure of some of the world's largest retailers. Working on implementations for Staples.com, Kohls Global Inventory Visibility, and Williams Sonoma's DTC channel gave me a practical understanding of how enterprise-scale product requirements translate into system design decisions.

The rigour of that consulting background — designing for millions of orders, handling inventory at global scale, managing implementations across teams of 5–10 engineers — shaped how I think about product complexity. When I moved into product ownership, I understood the technical constraints that most PMs never encounter directly. That makes for better requirements, more credible stakeholder conversations, and faster delivery.
